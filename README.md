# Pablo Lichtig's website

Welcome to my page! I'll try to keep it updated with my work, CV and recent scientific work.

## Current work
I am currently a researcher in the Argentinean National Commission for Atomic Energy (CNEA), in the Atmospheric Chemistry division of the Environmental Chemistry department.

### Expertise
I have specialized in Air Quality Research over South America, both from a modeling and observational perspective.

#### Modeling work
I have worked with MUSICAv0 and WRF-Chem over South America. In this research, we have found that about half of the carbon monoxide in South America is derived from natural biogenic emissions of VOC's, and that there is a strong influence from central African biomass burning in all of the continent, including the southern part.

On the other hand, South America's biomass burning also has a strong effect not only in tropical area's, but also in the southern tip of Africa.

#### Observational work.
I was part of the ARCAL-RLA7023 project to measure and characterize PM2.5 in latin american megacities, as part of the CNEA team. I was in charge of organizing part of the measurements in Buenos Aires. This resulted in a publication in which the complete composition of PM2.5 in the city is analyzed, where we found that organic matter represents 40-60% of the total mass of these particles. Traffic represented the major source of particles in the city.

## Studies
I did a "Licenciatura" (roughly equivalent to a Master's degree) in the University of Buenos Aires, Argentina, and graduated in 2018.
After some time, I got my PhD in Science and Technology-Chemistry from the University of San Martín's Institute for Environmental Research and Engeneering, under the supervision of Laura Dawidowski and Darío Gómez (both have been members of IPCC's Task Force for Inventories, and coauthors of the 2006 Guidelines), doing the research in CNEA.

I then did a 1 year Postdoc in the NSF National Center for Atmospheric Research (NSF-NCAR), under the supervision of Dr. Gabriele Pfister, before coming back to Argentina as a researcher.
